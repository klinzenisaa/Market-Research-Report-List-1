<p><ul><li>Medical Refrigerator (2℃-8℃)</li><li>Blood Refrigerator (4℃-±1℃)</li><li>Medical Freezer (-10℃--25℃)</li><li>Medical Cryopreservation Box (-10℃--50℃)</li><li>Medical Cryogenic Storage Box (-50℃--150℃)</li><li>Liquid Nitrogen Tank (-150℃--196℃)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biomedical cryogenic storage equipment market includes various types of storage solutions catering to different temperature requirements. Medical refrigerators maintain temperatures between 2°C to 8°C for preserving pharmaceuticals. Blood refrigerators operate at 4°C with a ±1°C tolerance for blood products. Medical freezers operate at -10°C to -25°C, while cryopreservation boxes store samples at -10°C to -50°C. Medical cryogenic storage boxes function between -50°C and -150°C, and liquid nitrogen tanks manage ultra-low temperatures from -150°C to -196°C for biological materials.</p></p>
