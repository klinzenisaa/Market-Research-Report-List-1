<p><p>The non-invasive cancer diagnostics market encompasses various approaches, including circulating tumor cells (CTCs), circulating tumor nucleic acids (ctNAs), and exosomes. CTCs are cancer cells that detach from tumors and circulate in the bloodstream, enabling early detection and monitoring of disease progression. ctNAs include DNA and RNA fragments shed by tumors, providing valuable genetic information for personalized treatments. Exosomes are small vesicles released by cancer cells that carry molecular markers, aiding in diagnosis and understanding tumor biology. Together, these technologies enhance cancer detection and management.</p></p>
