Three-piece cans are constructed from three separate parts—two cylindrical sides and a base, often welded together, making them suitable for various food products. In contrast, two-piece cans are made from a single sheet, formed into a can body with a separate lid, allowing for lightweight designs and enhanced durability. Both types serve distinct applications, catering to diverse consumer needs in packaging.</p></p>
