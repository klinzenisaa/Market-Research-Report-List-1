<p><p>The medical-grade hand sanitizer market is broadly categorized into two types: alcohol-based and non-alcoholic formulations. Alcohol-based hand sanitizers typically contain a high concentration of isopropyl alcohol or ethanol, effectively killing pathogens and providing quick disinfection. They are favored in clinical settings for their efficacy. Non-alcoholic hand sanitizers, often using antimicrobial agents like benzalkonium chloride, offer an alternative for those sensitive to alcohol. While generally less effective against certain viruses, they provide a milder option for frequent use, especially in non-medical environments.</p></p>
